What is the BMI?

Body mass index, commonly reffered to as BMI is value that is derived from the weight of an individual divided by their height squared. It is expressed in units of kg/m². While this metric tends to be flawed for individuals with a significant amount of muscle mass as opposed to fat, it is an adequate measure for most of the population. Based on an individuals's results, they will either be considered underweight, normal weight, overweight, or obese.
